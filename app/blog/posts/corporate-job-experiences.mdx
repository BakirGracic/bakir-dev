Working for a company is no easy feat, to say the least, that may not be suitable for everyone! But it sure is a great starting point and a vast field to hone skills and gather more experience in your niche.

## The pros

- **_Work Ethic_**: To know that your salary and many other things depend on "[The Art of Showing Up](https://www.amazon.com/Art-Showing-Up-Yourself-People/dp/1615196617)", at some point will impact your discipline and work ethic and will surely get you in "the machine"
- **_Company Vision_**: Your primary goal should be to follow your company's vision and mission. For example a Marketing agency has the mission to provide best services to customers, and that requires passionate and meaningful work, while simultaneously excelling yourself! (_pro tip: have the mindest of following the "3 visions rule": vision of the company, vision of the client, team vision of solving the problem. Employers love this!_)
- **_Other People_**: Most of the time you won't work alone! You'll either have those above you, your team, or both! And that is a fabulous opportunity to practice soft skills, communication and teamwork! For the new "TikTok" generation, if they accept their role seriously, that may help in social akwardness, stuttering, introvertism, etc.
- **_Skills_**: Having all set up, the only thing left to do, that is easier said than done, is the actual work! During the execution process, unforgettable skills and experiences are forged. Having that in mind, you ought to choose a company/role that suits ALL of your needs and expectations!

## The Cons
- **_Boss_**: Even tho some people may find comfort and success in working "standard" jobs, while others, like myself, may not! Working for a company may limit you with time, opportunites, and the factor that you may not be able to achieve your ideas, not to speak about personal businesses, etc.
- **_Problems_**: There will be constant problems, whether you believe it (see it), or not! Some may not impact you, but others will. Have understanding that people are under pressure, have various kinds of other probelems and may start some disputes. But don't be afraid to speak up against injustice and exploitation!
- **_Enjoyment_**: If your workplace is monotone and simple, with not much activity and change - it may bore you immensly over time. For those who want a more dynamic work environment it might be a potentian problem. No wonder today's job offers mention "dynamic work enviroment", think about it!

## Conclusion

As you could see, there are many pro's and con's regarding corporate jobs. The right thing to do is to decide for yourself, based on your needs and expectations, what suits you the most. Please don't follow, at least word-for-word, "life gurus" since that is a nicely packaged scam and exploitation! Rather listen to yourself, follow you passion and be yourself!

For IT rookies and other computer related niches, I would suggest you to gain as much skills from educating yourself, learning from personal projects and freelancing. Then I recommend working for a couple of companies for max 2 years, be sure to learn from those experiences as much as possible. After that, you may make your own product/service/business/company, or balance having both the job and something of yours.

Good luck, don't forget to focus and put in that work!
